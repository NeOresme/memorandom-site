Setup

  1. Shuffle cards thoroughly.
  2. Each player gets two cards (“Personal Cards”). Players may look at their own Personal Cards anytime, but other players shouldn’t see them.
  3. Nine cards are laid face down in a 3×3 grid (“The Grid”).

Rules

  1. Turns go clockwise from the dealer’s left.
  2. On your turn, do one of the following:
    • Sneak: Look at any 3 cards in The Grid without anyone else seeing (look once each).
    • Shuffle: Swap the positions of 2 or 3 cards in The Grid without looking.
    • Swap: Swap 1 Grid card with 1 Personal Card (don’t look before switching).
    • Call “MemoRandom!”
  3. Calling “MemoRandom!” (at the beginning of your turn, when you think you have a winning hand):
    • Each player chooses a row, column, or diagonal of 3 cards in The Grid (choices locked before flip).
    • Chosen 3 cards + Personal Cards form each player’s hand (see Difficulty).
    • Flip all Grid + Personal Cards and score (see Scoring).

Difficulty

Easy: 3 (row/col/diag) + 1 personal card

Medium: 3 (row/col/diag) + 2 personal cards

Scoring

  1. Play to a desired point cap (suggested: 5 points).
  2. A set is a 5-card hand of one:
    • color
    • shape
    • number
  3. If the caller has a set: caller +1; others +1 if they also have a set.
  4. If the caller does not have a set: caller −1; others +1 if they have a set.

Notes (house rules welcome)

  • Keep Personal Cards face down unless looking; keep them in the same place for tracking swaps.
  • If card backs have orientation, keep all cards oriented the same way to avoid accidental clues.
  • Wildcards: 3 wildcards (and one QR card) may be included—keep or remove to your liking.
  • Decide if note-taking is allowed.
